Project Summary  by Hao Sun, P.Eng., LC, IESNA

For the new KPL project, we worked with LGA Architects during the design stage to put Art, Science and Human Experience into the lighting design. The lighting system is the product of ambient luminescence, focal glow and sparkle lighting, which come together to meet the LEED and ASHRAE 90.1 requirements.

As per the selection of the lighting fixtures and arrangements, we used layers of several lighting systems in the spaces, rather than utilizing only one type of the luminaire system. General lighting is provided by the Metalumen Armatura SD1 linear system in the information service area. The SPI Echo Wall Sconce provides focal glow to the columns and ceiling in order to spatially fill the exposed structural ceiling space. The design target for the book shelves is set at avg. 50fc at 2.5fc above the finished floor and avg. 30fc on the vertical surface of the book racks. The lighting level obtained/measured after the installation matches our design goals.

A similar concept was used in other areas, such as the reading lounge. The ceiling lighting system consists of LED pot lights, fibre optical twinkle lights, and T8 cove lights above the drop ceiling. The SPI echo system is installed on columns in order to increase the spacious volume. The SPI echo mounted toward the Mural wall intends to highlight the 24 ft high and 45 ft wide art display. The design level in the reading lounge area is on avg. 40fc at the working plane.

Overall, the purpose for the lighting design is to create an environment in which the reader will feel comfortable and relaxed when in the library, combining both function and art.
