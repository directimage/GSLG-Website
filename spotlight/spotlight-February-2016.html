Norwich Administration Building by The Ventin Group Ltd.

Local architecture often symbolizes the values and spirit of a community. The architectural concept for the new Norwich Administration Building seeks to reflect the qualities and characteristics that define this rural community, with contemporary, refined rustic details and an open, transparent image. The modernized rustic building celebrates Norwich history and connects to community roots, while still articulating an optimistic vision of the future.
     Inside, the two-storey, open office area has a gable roof with a barnboard-clad feature wall representing the original tobacco kilns once common in the Township. Barnboard-clad cross support beams and columns further distinguish the barn-like structure, with white walls to provide a modern contrast to the rustic ambience, and an imitation 'hay loft' window above the lobby end-wall.
     The entry lobby and reception areas are open and spacious, with a barrier-free accessible counter which overlooks the Council Chamber. The Chamber is enclosed by a folding glass wall that opens up onto the Lobby to accommodate larger meetings and community exhibitions. The perimeter of the building interior is arranged with private staff offices and a lunchroom, with a central, fire-proof storage vault for the Municipality's permanent records.
     The exterior of the building is clad in rough-cut stone on the west elevation, where the circular Council Chamber rises to portray a silo with tall vertical slot windows.
